🖼️ Task 1 — Edit Your Photos & Scans in Photoshop
Goal: Every page you photograph or scan should look clean, well-lit, and true to the original work — white paper should look white, colours should be accurate.

Capture Your Work First

🖨️ Scan at College

  • Place page flat on the scanner glass, close the lid gently
  • Swipe your student card → choose Scan to Email
  • Set resolution to 300 dpi
  • Download from your inbox to a drive or desktop folder

📸 Photograph Your Pages

  • Use soft daylight — avoid direct sun or yellow indoor bulbs
  • Lay the sketchbook flat, stand or mount your phone above it
  • Take several shots and pick the most evenly lit one
  • Photos capture surface texture that scanners miss
Best results: scan for clarity, photograph for texture — combine both if you can.

Edit in Photoshop — click each step to open

1
Open and Duplicate
▶
  • Open your image in Photoshop
  • Drag the Background layer onto the New Layer icon in the Layers panel
  • This keeps a safe original beneath your edits — always do this first
2
Balance Tone — Levels & Curves
▶
  • Image → Adjustments → Levels Ctrl/Cmd+L
  • Drag the black and white input sliders until the histogram just touches both ends — this adds contrast and makes white paper look white
  • Then Image → Adjustments → Curves Ctrl/Cmd+M
  • Make a gentle S-curve: lift the lights slightly, anchor the darks
Why? Levels recover brightness; Curves add depth. Apply these before any colour correction.
3
Correct Colour — Camera Raw Filter
▶
  • Filter → Camera Raw Filter…
  • Set White Balance to Custom
  • Adjust Temperature (left = cooler / right = warmer) and Tint until paper looks clean white
  • Avoid over-brightening — aim for natural, not glowing
4
Place into A3 Layout
▶
  • Select with Marquee Tool (M) → Ctrl/Cmd+C then Ctrl/Cmd+V
  • Or use File → Place Embedded… to insert the whole page as a new layer
  • Resize with Ctrl/Cmd+T (Free Transform) — hold Shift to constrain proportions
5
Save Your Work
▶
  • Save as .PSD — keeps all layers editable
  • Export as .JPG — for layout and upload: File → Export → Save for Web (Legacy) → JPEG, 80–90% quality
  • Rename clearly: firstname_lastname_portfolioweek_page1.jpg

The Organic Layout Process — click each step

1
Create a Background Texture with Content-Aware Fill
▶
  • Use the Rectangular Marquee Tool (M) to select a visually interesting area of your image — a texture, pattern, or detail
  • Ctrl/Cmd+C then Ctrl/Cmd+V — creates a new layer with just that area
  • Hide the original layer by clicking the 👁 eye icon
  • Use the Magic Wand Tool (W) to select the white background area
  • Go to Edit → Content-Aware Fill — Photoshop fills the space using patterns from your image, creating an organic, textured background
2
Lighten the Background with the Dodge Tool
▶
  • Merge your layers: Ctrl/Cmd+E
  • Select the Dodge Tool (O)
  • Set Range to Midtones · large soft brush · 20–30% Exposure
  • Sweep across the whole page in broad strokes to create a light, airy background that won't compete with your content
Brush shortcuts: [ to decrease size · ] to increase size
3
Place Images at Varied Scales
▶
  • Place your most important image first — make it the largest element
  • Add supporting images at progressively smaller sizes to guide the viewer's eye
  • Use Ctrl/Cmd+T to resize each layer
  • Let the background texture suggest where things want to sit — follow it rather than forcing a grid
Key principle: larger = more important. Vary scales deliberately to create visual hierarchy.
4
Fine-Tune Alignment
▶
  • Arrow keys for precise 1px nudging
  • Shift + Arrow to move 10px at a time
  • Align edges, centres, or use the Rule of Thirds as a loose guide — but trust your eye over the grid
5
Add Subtle Depth with Drop Shadows
▶
  • Click a layer → Layer Style → Drop Shadow (or the fx icon in the Layers panel)
  • Keep shadows subtle: Opacity 30–50% · Distance 5–10px · Size 5–10px · Angle 120°
  • Apply to all content layers for consistent depth
6
Export Each Page as JPEG
▶
  • In Photoshop: File → Export → Export As… → JPEG, maximum quality
  • Name each file clearly: firstname_lastname_page1.jpg, page2.jpg etc.
  • Keep all your JPEGs in one folder — you'll upload them all at once in the next step
7
Build Your PDF — use the PDF Builder tool
▶

Use the Portfolio PDF Builder to compile your JPEG pages into a single PDF ready for submission. It runs in your browser — nothing is uploaded anywhere.

  • Drop all your JPEG pages in at once — they sort by filename automatically
  • Drag to reorder if needed
  • Enter your name and student ID — this names the file for you
  • Click Download PDF — then open it to check it looks right before submitting
Important: your submission must be a .pdf file — not a folder of images, not a PSD. Open the downloaded file in Acrobat or Preview to confirm before uploading.
